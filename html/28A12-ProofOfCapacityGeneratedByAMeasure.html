proof of capacity generated by a measure


For a finite measure space (X,ℱ,μ), define

μ*:𝒫⁢(X)→ℝ+,
μ*⁢(S)=inf⁡{μ⁢(A):A∈ℱ,A⊇S}.

We show that μ* is an ℱ-capacity and that a subset S⊆X is (ℱ,μ*)-capacitable (http://planetmath.org/ChoquetCapacity) if and only if it is in the completion (http://planetmath.org/CompleteMeasure) of ℱ with respect to μ.

Note, first of all, that μ*⁢(S)=μ⁢(S) for any S∈ℱ. That μ* is increasing follows directly from the definition. If An∈ℱ is a decreasing sequence of sets then A=⋂nAn is also in ℱ and, by continuity from above (http://planetmath.org/PropertiesForMeasure) for measures,

μ*⁢(An)=μ⁢(An)→μ⁢(A)=μ*⁢(A)

as n→∞.

Now suppose that Sn is an increasing sequence of subsets of X and set S=⋃nSn. Then, μ*⁢(Sn)≤μ*⁢(S) for each n and, hence, limn→∞⁡μ*⁢(Sn)≤μ*⁢(S).

To prove the reverse inequality, choose any ϵ>0 and sequence An∈ℱ with Sn⊆An and μ⁢(An)≤μ*⁢(Sn)+2-n⁢ϵ. Then, Am∩An⊇Sn whenever m≥n and, therefore,

μ⁢(An∖Am)=μ⁢(An)-μ⁢(Am∩An)≤μ⁢(An)-μ*⁢(Sn)≤2-n⁢ϵ.

Additivity of μ then gives

μ⁢(⋃m≤nAm)≤μ⁢(An)+∑m=1n-1μ⁢(Am∖An)≤μ*⁢(Sn)+ϵ.

So, by continuity from below for measures,

μ*⁢(S)≤μ⁢(⋃nAn)=limn→∞⁡μ⁢(⋃m≤nAm)≤limn→∞⁡μ*⁢(Sn)+ϵ.

Choosing ϵ arbitrarily small shows that μ*⁢(Sn)→μ*⁢(S) and, therefore, μ* is indeed an ℱ-capacity.

Now suppose that S is in the completion of ℱ with respect to μ, so that there exists A,B∈ℱ with A⊆S⊆B and μ⁢(B∖A)=0. Then,

μ*⁢(A)=μ⁢(A)=μ⁢(B)≥μ*⁢(S)

and S is indeed (ℱ,μ*)-capacitable. Conversely, let S be (ℱ,μ*)-capacitable. Then, there exists An,Bn∈ℱ such that An⊆S⊆Bn and

μ⁢(An)≥μ*⁢(S)-1/n,μ⁢(Bn)≤μ*⁢(S)+1/n.

Setting A=⋃nAn and B=⋂nBn gives A⊆S⊆B and

μ⁢(A)≥μ*⁢(S)≥μ⁢(B).

So μ⁢(B∖A)=μ⁢(B)-μ⁢(A)=0, as required.
