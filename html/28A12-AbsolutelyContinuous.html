absolutely continuous


Let μ and ν be signed measures or complex measures on the same measurable space (Ω,𝒮). We say that ν is absolutely continuous with respect to μ if, for each A∈𝒮 such that |μ|⁢(A)=0, it holds that ν⁢(A)=0. This is usually denoted by ν≪μ.

Remarks.

If μ and ν are signed measures and (ν+,ν-) is the Jordan decomposition of ν, the following are equivalent:

  1. 1.

    ν≪μ;

  2. 2.

    ν+≪μ and ν-≪μ;

  3. 3.

    |ν|≪|μ|.

If ν is a finite signed or complex measure and ν≪μ, the following useful property holds: for each ε>0, there is a δ>0 such that |ν|⁢(E)<ε whenever |μ|⁢(E)<δ.
