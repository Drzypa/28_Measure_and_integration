proof of dominated convergence theorem


It is not difficult to prove that f is measurable. In fact we can write

f⁢(x)=supn⁡infk≥n⁡fk⁢(x)

and we know that measurable functions are closed under the sup and inf operation.

Consider the sequence gn⁢(x)=2⁢Φ⁢(x)-|f⁢(x)-fn⁢(x)|. Clearly gn are nonnegative functions since f-fn≤2⁢Φ. So, applying Fatou’s Lemma, we obtain

limn→∞⁡∫X|f-fn|⁢𝑑μ≤lim supn→∞⁡∫X|f-fn|⁢𝑑μ
= -lim infn→∞⁡∫X-|f-fn|⁢d⁢μ
= ∫X2⁢Φ⁢𝑑μ-lim infn→∞⁡∫X2⁢Φ-|f-fn|⁢d⁢μ
≤ ∫X2⁢Φ⁢𝑑μ-∫X2⁢Φ-lim supn→∞⁡|f-fn|⁢d⁢μ
= ∫X2⁢Φ⁢𝑑μ-∫X2⁢Φ⁢𝑑μ=0.
