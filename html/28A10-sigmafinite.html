σ-finite


A measure space (Ω,ℬ,μ) is a finite measure space if μ⁢(Ω)<∞; it is σ-finite if the total space is the union of a finite or countable family of sets of finite measure, i.e. if there exists a countable set ℱ⊂ℬ such that μ⁢(A)<∞ for each A∈ℱ, and Ω=⋃A∈ℱA. In this case we also say that μ is a σ-finite measure. If μ is not σ-finite, we say that it is σ-infinite.

Examples. Any finite measure space is σ-finite. A more interesting example is the Lebesgue measure μ in ℝn: it is σ-finite but not finite. In fact

ℝn=⋃k∈ℕ[-k,k]n

([-k,k]n is a cube with center at 0 and side length 2⁢k, and its measure is (2⁢k)n), but μ⁢(ℝn)=∞.
