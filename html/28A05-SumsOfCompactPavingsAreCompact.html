sums of compact pavings are compact


Suppose that (Ki,𝒦i) is a paved space for each i in an index set I. The direct sum, or disjoint union (http://planetmath.org/DisjointUnion), ∑i∈IKi is the union of the disjoint sets Ki×{i}. The direct sum of the paving 𝒦i is defined as

∑i∈I𝒦i={∑i∈ISi:Si∈𝒦i∪{∅}⁢ is empty for all but finitely many ⁢i}.
Theorem.

Let (Ki,Ki) be compact paved spaces for i∈I. Then, ∑iKi is a compact paving on ∑iKi.

The paving 𝒦′ consisting of subsets of ∑i𝒦i of the form ∑iSi where Si=∅ for all but a single i∈I is easily shown to be compact. Indeed, if 𝒦′′⊆𝒦′ satisfies the finite intersection property then there is an i∈I such that S⊆Ki×{i} for every S∈𝒦′′. Compactness of 𝒦i gives ⋂𝒦′′≠∅.

Then, as ∑i𝒦i consists of finite unions of sets in 𝒦′, it is a compact paving (see compact pavings are closed subsets of a compact space).
