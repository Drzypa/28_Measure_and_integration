Argand diagram


An Argand diagram is the graphical representation of complex numbers written in polar coordinates. For example, if z∈ℂ is a complex number, then z can be written as r⁢ei⁢θ, where r is the length of z considered as a vector (x,y)∈ℝ2, with z=x+y⁢i, and θ is the value such that tan⁡θ=yx, and can be interpreted as the angle z makes with the x-axis.
