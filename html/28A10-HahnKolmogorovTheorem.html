Hahn-Kolmogorov theorem


Let 𝒜0 be an algebra of subsets of a set X. If a finitely additive measure μ0:𝒜→ℝ∪{∞} satisfies

μ0⁢(⋃n=1∞An)=∑n=1∞μ0⁢(An)

for any disjoint family {An:n∈ℕ} of elements of 𝒜0 such that ∪n=0∞An∈𝒜0, then μ0 extends to a measure defined on the σ-algebra 𝒜 generated by 𝒜0; i.e. there exists a measure μ:𝒜→ℝ∪{∞} such that its restriction (http://planetmath.org/RestrictionOfAFunction) to 𝒜0 coincides with μ0.

If μ0 is σ-finite (http://planetmath.org/SigmaFinite), then the extension is unique.
