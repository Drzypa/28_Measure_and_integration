centre of mass of half-disc


Let E be the upper half-disc of the disc  x2+y2≦R  in ℝ2 with a surface-density 1. By the symmetry, its centre of mass lies on its medium radius, and therefore we only have to calculate the ordinate Y of the centre of mass. For doing that, one can use the double integral

Y=1ν⁢(E)⁢∬Ey⁢𝑑x⁢𝑑y,

where  ν⁢(E)=π⁢R22  is the area of the half-disc. The region of integration is defined by

E={(x,y)∈ℝ2⋮-R≦x≦R, 0≦y≦R2-x2}.

Accordingly we may write

Y=2π⁢R2⁢∫-RR𝑑x⁢∫0R2-x2y⁢𝑑y=2π⁢R2⁢∫-RRR2-x22⁢𝑑x=2π⁢R2⁢/x=-RR⁡(R2⁢x2-x36)=4⁢R3⁢π.

Thus the centre of mass is the point  (0,4⁢R3⁢π).
