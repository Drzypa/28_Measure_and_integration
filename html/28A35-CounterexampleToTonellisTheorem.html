counter-example to Tonelli’s theorem


The following observation demonstrates the necessity of the σ-finite assumption in Tonelli’s and Fubini’s theorem. Let X denote the closed unit interval [0,1] equipped with Lebesgue measure and Y the same set, but this time equipped with counting measure ν. Let

f⁢(x,y)={1 if ⁢x=y,0 otherwise.

Observe that

∫Y(∫Xf⁢(x,y)⁢𝑑μ⁢(x))⁢𝑑ν⁢(y)=0,

while

∫X(∫Yf⁢(x,y)⁢𝑑ν⁢(y))⁢𝑑μ⁢(x)=1.

The iterated integrals do not give the same value, this despite the fact that the integrand is a non-negative function.

Also observe that there does not exist a simple function on X×Y that is dominated by f. Hence,

∫X×Yf(x,y)d(μ(x)×ν(y)=0.

Therefore, the integrand is L1 integrable relative to the product measure. However, as we observed above, the iterated integrals do not agree. This observation illustrates the need for the σ-finite assumption for Fubini’s theorem.
