proof of existence and unicity of self-similar fractals


We consider the space 𝒦⁢(X)={K⊂X:K⁢compact⁢and⁢non⁢empty} endowed with the Hausdorff distance δ. Since Hausdorff metric inherits completeness, being X complete, (𝒦⁢(X),δ) is complete too. We then consider the mapping T:𝒦⁢(X)→𝒦⁢(X) defined by

T⁢(A)=⋃i=1NTi⁢(A).

We claim that T is a contraction. In fact, recalling that δ⁢(A1∪A2,B1∪B2)≤max⁡{δ⁢(A1,B1),δ⁢(A2,B2)} while δ⁢(Ti⁢(A),Ti⁢(B))≤λi⁢δ⁢(A,B) if Ti is λi-Lipschitz, we have

δ⁢(T⁢(A),T⁢(B)) =δ⁢(⋃iTi⁢(A),⋃iTi⁢(B))≤maxi⁡δ⁢(Ti⁢(A),Ti⁢(B))
≤maxi⁡λi⁢δ⁢(A,B)=λ⁢δ⁢(A,B)

with λ=maxi⁡λi<1.

So T is a contraction on the complete metric space 𝒦⁢(X) and hence, by Banach Fixed Point Theorem, there exists one and only one K∈𝒦⁢(X) such that T⁢(K)=K.
