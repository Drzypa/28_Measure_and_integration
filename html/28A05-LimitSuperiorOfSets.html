limit superior of sets


Let A1,A2,… be a sequence of sets. The limit superior of sets is defined by

lim sup⁡An=⋂n=1∞⋃k=n∞Ak.

It is easy to see that x∈lim sup⁡An if and only if x∈An for infinitely many values of n. Because of this, in probability theory the notation [An⁢i.o.] is often used to refer to lim sup⁡An, where i.o. stands for infinitely often.

The limit inferior of sets is defined by

lim inf⁡An=⋃n=1∞⋂k=n∞Ak,

and it can be shown that x∈lim inf⁡An if and only if x belongs to An for all but finitely many values of n.
