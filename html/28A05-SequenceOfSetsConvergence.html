sequence of sets convergence


Let {An}n=1∞ be a sequence of sets, and A a set.

The sequence {An}n=1∞ is said to from below to A, (shortly, An↑A or An↗A), iff
1) An⊆An+1  ∀n≥1
2) A=⋃n=1∞An

The sequence {An}n=1∞ is said to from above to A, (shortly, An↓A or An↘A), iff
1) An+1⊆An  ∀n≥1
2) A=⋂n=1∞An

In both cases the less accurate notation

A=limn⟶∞⁡An

is also used.
