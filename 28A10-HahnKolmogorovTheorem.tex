Let $\mathscr{A}_0$ be an algebra of subsets of a set $X$. If a finitely additive measure $\mu_0\colon \mathscr{A}\to\R\cup \{\infty\}$ satisfies
$$\mu_0(\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty A_n) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \mu_0(A_n)$$
for any disjoint family $\{A_n:n\in \N\}$ of elements of $\mathscr{A}_0$ such that $\cup_{n=0}^\infty A_n\in \mathscr{A}_0$, then $\mu_0$ 
extends to a measure defined on the $\sigma$-algebra $\mathscr{A}$ generated by $\mathscr{A}_0$; i.e. there exists a measure $\mu\colon\mathscr{A}\to \R\cup\{\infty\}$ such that its \PMlinkname{restriction}{RestrictionOfAFunction} to $\mathscr{A}_0$ coincides with $\mu_0$.

If $\mu_0$ is \PMlinkname{$\sigma$-finite}{SigmaFinite}, then the extension is unique.
